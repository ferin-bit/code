Introduction

Mental health is a crucial aspect of overall health and well-being. It encompasses our emotional, psychological, and social well-being, influencing how we think, feel, and act [15]. Depression, a common mental disorder, can significantly impact daily life and requires attention and support [2, 5].

What is Depression?

Depression, also known as major depressive disorder or clinical depression, is more than just feeling sad. It's a serious illness that can cause persistent symptoms affecting mood, thoughts, and daily activities like sleeping, eating, and working [5].

Symptoms of Depression

  • Persistent sadness or low mood
  • Loss of interest or pleasure in activities
  • Changes in appetite or weight
  • Sleep disturbances (insomnia or oversleeping)
  • Fatigue or loss of energy
  • Feelings of worthlessness or guilt
  • Difficulty concentrating or making decisions
  • Thoughts of death or suicide [5]

If you or someone you know is experiencing these symptoms, it's important to seek help [5].

Mental Health Resources

  • National Alliance on Mental Illness (NAMI): Provides support, education, and advocacy for individuals and families affected by mental illness [8].
  • National Institute of Mental Health (NIMH): Conducts research on mental disorders and promotes mental health awareness [5, 16].
  • MentalHealth.gov: Offers information and resources on mental health conditions and where to find help [8].
  • World Health Organization (WHO): Provides global information and resources on mental health [3].
  • 988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline: Call or text 988 in the US and Canada to connect with trained counselors who can provide support in a crisis [11].

How to Help Someone with Depression

  • Encourage them to seek professional help [5].
  • Listen without judgement and offer support [11].
  • Help them find resources and support groups [11].
  • Remind them that they are not alone and that recovery is possible [11].
  • Be patient and understanding [11].
